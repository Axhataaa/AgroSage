Quick Answers

Common questions before you start

I don't have a soil test. Can I still use it?
Yes. Use average values for your region as a starting point. The recommendations are still useful as a guide, even without lab-precise numbers.
How accurate is the crop recommendation?
The model achieves ~94% accuracy on test data. Treat it as a well-informed suggestion — your local knowledge still matters.
Which crops does Disease Check support?
It covers multiple plant families — including tomato, potato, corn, grape, apple, and more — trained across disease categories sourced from several agricultural datasets.
Is this free to use?
Completely free. No sign-up required to try the crop advisor — create an account only if you want to save your results.
About This Project

Built with real data, for real decisions

AgroSage is a machine-learning web application designed to help farmers and students make better agricultural decisions — using soil science, live weather, and computer vision.

🌾
Crop Recommendation Model
Random Forest classifier trained on the Crop Recommendation Dataset. Analyses 7 parameters (N, P, K, pH, temperature, humidity, rainfall) across 22 crop classes. Test accuracy: ~94%.
🔬
Disease Detection Model
CNN (MobileNetV2 transfer learning) trained on a combination of multiple agricultural image datasets. Classifies disease and healthy states across several plant families from a single leaf photograph.
🌤️
Live Weather Integration
Powered by Open-Meteo API — free, no API key required. Fetches real-time temperature, humidity, and rainfall for any city worldwide via geocoding.
Stack: Python · Flask · TensorFlow · Scikit-learn · SQLite · Vanilla JS

🧪 Parameter Guide

Soil Analysis & Parameter Guide

Understanding your soil's nutrient profile is the foundation of successful farming. Here's what each parameter means and how it affects crop growth.

🟢
Nitrogen (N)
Promotes Leaf Growth
Low (0 kg/ha)Ideal: 50–100High (150 kg/ha)
Essential for chlorophyll synthesis and leaf development. Ideal: 50–100 kg/ha. Too low causes yellowing (chlorosis); too high promotes excessive vegetative growth and delays fruiting.
🟠
Phosphorus (P)
Root & Fruit Development
Low (0 kg/ha)Ideal: 25–50High (80 kg/ha)
Critical for root strength, seed formation, and energy transfer. Ideal: 25–50 kg/ha. Deficiency leads to dark purpling of leaves and severely stunted root systems.
🟣
Potassium (K)
Disease Resistance
Low (0 kg/ha)Ideal: 40–80High (120 kg/ha)
Regulates water balance, stomatal function, and disease immunity. Ideal: 40–80 kg/ha. Deficiency causes leaf scorch at margins, weak stems, and poor grain fill.
🟡
Soil pH
Nutrient Availability
Acidic (0)Ideal: 6.0–7.5Alkaline (14)
Controls nutrient solubility and microbial activity. Ideal: 6.0–7.5 for most crops. Below 5.5 locks up phosphorus; above 7.5 reduces availability of iron, manganese, and zinc.
🌡️
Temperature
Growth Rate Regulator
Low (0°C)Ideal: 20–35°CHigh (50°C)
Determines germination success and metabolic rate. Most crops prefer 20–35°C. Below 10°C slows germination; above 40°C causes heat stress and inhibits enzymatic activity.
💧
Humidity & Rainfall
Water Stress Index
Low (0%/mm)Ideal: 50–80% / 50–200mmHigh (100%/300mm)
Humidity of 50–80% and annual rainfall of 50–200 mm suits most field crops. Rice requires 150–300 mm; drought-tolerant crops like mustard and mothbeans thrive below 75 mm.
💡
Soil Improvement Tips
🌿
Nitrogen Deficiency
Cause: Low organic matter, excessive leaching, or sandy soils.
Symptoms: Uniform yellowing (chlorosis) starting from older leaves; stunted growth and pale green foliage.
Solution: Apply urea (46% N) or ammonium nitrate at 50–100 kg/ha. Grow leguminous cover crops (lentil, clover, chickpea) to fix atmospheric nitrogen naturally.
🧡
Phosphorus Deficiency
Cause: Acidic soil (pH < 5.5), low organic matter, or excessive soil moisture.
Symptoms: Dark purple or reddish discolouration on leaf undersides; poor root development and delayed maturity.
Solution: Apply single superphosphate (SSP) or DAP at 25–50 kg/ha. Raise soil pH above 5.5 to unlock phosphorus already present in the soil.
💦
Potassium Deficiency
Cause: Sandy or heavily leached soils; over-irrigation or excessive rainfall.
Symptoms: Scorch or brown burning at leaf margins and tips; weak stems prone to lodging; poor grain and fruit fill.
Solution: Apply muriate of potash (MOP, 60% K₂O) or sulfate of potash at 30–60 kg/ha. Wood ash is a natural K source. Avoid over-irrigation which accelerates leaching.
🧂
Alkaline Soil (High pH)
Cause: Calcareous parent material, irrigation with hard water, or excessive lime application.
Symptoms: Iron and zinc chlorosis (interveinal yellowing on young leaves); reduced phosphorus and micronutrient uptake despite adequate fertilisation.
Solution: Apply elemental sulfur (200–500 kg/ha) or acidifying fertilizers such as ammonium sulfate. Incorporate compost or peat moss to gradually lower pH toward 6.0–7.0.

🔍 Key Insights from This Data
🌱
Soil pH is the top predictor
pH carries the highest feature importance in the trained model — a field with pH above 7.5 or below 5.5 will significantly limit viable crop options regardless of nutrients.
💧
Rice needs 3× more rainfall than wheat
Rice averages 225 mm/yr vs. wheat's 75 mm/yr. Choosing between them based on local rainfall alone can double or halve irrigation costs in a season.
🌡️
Temperature splits two clear crop groups
Crops cluster into cool-season (10–25°C: wheat, mustard, chickpea) and warm-season (22–38°C: rice, cotton, mango). Matching your month's temperature to the right group is the first filter to apply.
